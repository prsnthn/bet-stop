Signs of Problem Gambling

Recognizing the signs of problem gambling is the first step toward recovery. If you experience any of these symptoms, consider self-exclusion and professional help:

Financial Signs

  • Borrowing money to gamble
  • Missing bill payments due to betting
  • Selling possessions to fund gambling

Behavioral Signs

  • Gambling with increasing amounts
  • Unable to stop or cut back
  • Lying about gambling activities

Emotional Signs

  • Feeling restless when not gambling
  • Using gambling to escape problems
  • Experiencing guilt after gambling

Frequently Asked Questions About Gambling Self-Exclusion

What is gambling self-exclusion?

Self-exclusion is a tool that allows you to voluntarily ban yourself from gambling platforms for a set period. Once activated, operators must prevent you from accessing their betting services.

How long does self-exclusion last?

You can choose to self-exclude for 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, or permanently. Once set, the exclusion cannot be reversed until the chosen period expires.

What happens after I submit the form?

Your self-exclusion request will be sent directly to the selected betting operators. They will process your request and confirm via email. Most operators implement the exclusion within 24 hours.

Can I cancel my self-exclusion once it's active?

No, self-exclusion cannot be cancelled or reversed during the chosen period. This strict policy ensures the effectiveness of the program in helping people overcome gambling addiction.

What happens to my existing betting accounts?

When you self-exclude, operators will close or suspend your betting accounts. Any funds in your accounts should be returned to you, but winnings from bets placed after requesting exclusion may be voided.

Will self-exclusion affect my credit score?

No, self-exclusion does not affect your credit score or appear on your credit report. It's a private arrangement between you and the betting operators to help you stop gambling.
